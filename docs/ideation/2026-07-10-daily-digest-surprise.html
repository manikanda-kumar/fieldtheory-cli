Making the daily digest talk back

Bold, unexpected directions for daily synthesis & the second-brain loop — 2026-07-10
surprise-me mode repo-grounded 1 foundation + 7 surprises + 1 stretch engine: ft daily

Today's digest is a flat, passive, read-only list. It groups new saves into themes, then dumps 10+ unranked raw links under "connects to earlier saves" with no reason for the connection. interests.md is mechanical term-counting — its top "interests" are junk tokens like stars, code, tool. Nothing takes a stance, nothing resurfaces, nothing closes a loop, and the digest is never read back. Meanwhile a working TTS client sits unused. Every idea below turns one of those gaps into a feature.

Foundation — fix trust before delight

00

The trustworthy coverage footer

The digest claims to be your whole day. It silently sees ~5 of 6 sources. Make it confess.

What — A mechanical (never LLM) provenance footer on every digest: which sources were indexed and how fresh, which are dark, and whether limits were hit. "Coverage: X ✓ · Raindrop ✗ stale 8d · GitHub ✓ · YouTube ✓ · Projects ✓ · x-list & following not indexed · 200-item cap hit (14 dropped)." Optionally gate the digest on a freshness threshold and warn loudly when a source has gone silent.

Why it's the foundation — Every other idea assumes the digest is complete. It isn't, and worse, a partial digest looks identical to a full one — you can't tell a quiet day from a broken sync. This is the correctness fix that makes audio briefings, verdicts, and tension detection trustworthy. Not surprising; load-bearing.

Basis (verified in code): (1) rebuildCanonicalIndex() ingests only 5 families — X, Raindrop, GitHub-stars, Projects, YouTube (canonical-bookmarks-db.ts:662–700); x-list and following have zero canonical ingest. (2) Membership = first_saved_at ∈ [watermark, now) with MAX_ITEMS=200 newest-first, oldest truncated silently (daily/collect.ts). (3) Cross-source merge keeps the earliest date (:534), so re-engaging a known URL is invisible. (4) No dead-source check anywhere — a never-synced Raindrop just vanishes from output.

Effort: lowType: correctnessaxis: ingestion / trust

Related gaps this exposes, each a candidate fix in its own right: x-list + following ingest (2 dark sources), per-source watermark (late-landing items dropped), re-engagement surfacing (earliest-date masking), overflow signal (200-cap), null-date rescue (first_saved_at IS NULL = invisible forever).

Kept — ranked (the surprises)

01

The 3-minute morning briefing (audio)

Your digest, read to you while you make coffee — not a wall of text you'll skip.

What — After ft daily --write, pipe the themed digest through a short "radio host" script prompt and the existing TTS client to a dated library/daily/YYYY-MM-DD.mp3. A tight, spoken narration of the day's synthesis: three themes, the one connection that matters, one thing to act on. New ft daily --audio flag; nightly launchd writes it alongside the markdown.

Why it's the pick — Highest delight-per-effort. Text digests get skimmed or ignored; audio you consume passively. It reframes the second brain from "a file I grep" to "a companion that briefs me." Nothing else on this list changes the relationship to the tool this cheaply.

Basis (verified): createTtsClient().synthesize(text, path) already ships and is used for YouTube overviews — src/youtube/overview.ts:135–138, 286. Script-shaping prompt pattern exists in the same file. This is wiring, not new infra.
Effort: lowSurprise: highaxis: output format
02

A digest with a verdict (front page, not a feed)

One hero story above the fold. Every theme ends with an opinion.

What — Change the synthesis contract from "group into themes" to "edit a front page." Pick one hero connection for the day (biggest cross-source signal) and lead with it. Each theme closes with an opinionated call: "Read the Modal talk, skip the three rehashes" · "This contradicts Tuesday" · "Adjacent to your fieldtheory Now — worth 20 min." The LLM takes a stance instead of neutrally summarizing.

Why — The current digest gives every theme and every link equal weight, so nothing stands out and everything gets skimmed. Editorial hierarchy + a stance is what makes a briefing worth reading. Pure prompt-contract change — no new plumbing.

Basis: Prompt lives in buildDailyPrompt(), src/daily/synthesize.ts. Themes already carry summary + itemIds; add a verdict field + a hero flag and validate them the same way citations are validated today.
Effort: lowSurprise: medaxis: synthesis quality
03

The tension detector

Surface where your saves disagree — with each other, or with last week.

What — A dedicated pass that hunts for friction, not consensus: two items this week that argue opposite sides (pro-MCP vs. "skills > MCP"), or a new save that contradicts something you saved and endorsed weeks ago. A "⚡ Tensions" section: "You saved both 'model selectors are the future' and 'model selectors are limiting' this week — here's the split."

Why — Grouping similar things is what every feed reader does. Surfacing contradictions is what a thinking partner does — it's where learning actually happens, and it's genuinely differentiating. Directly usable against the connect-step data you already compute.

Basis: connect.ts already gives each new item its top-3 related past items via FTS. A second LLM sub-pass over those pairs asking "do these agree or conflict?" is the whole feature. Real signal exists in today's digest — MCP-vs-skills tension is sitting right there ungrouped.
Effort: medSurprise: highaxis: synthesis quality
04

Anti-recency: resurface the thread you dropped

Spaced repetition for bookmarks. The digest remembers what you forgot.

What — Each digest resurfaces one forgotten gem: a topic that was rising a few weeks ago then went silent, or a strong item saved N weeks back that connects to today and was never revisited. "Three weeks ago you were deep in local model training — 4 saves, then nothing. Today's vLLM-MoE save reopens it."

Why — A recency-only digest is a leaky bucket: you save, it scrolls, it's gone. Deliberate resurfacing is the difference between a log and a memory. It's the second-brain promise the current pipeline doesn't yet keep.

Basis: interests.ts already computes rising / steady / fading topic velocity (RECENT_DAYS=7 vs BASELINE_DAYS=30). "Fading but reactivated today" is a query over data already in hand — see the Fading block in current interests.md.
Effort: medSurprise: highaxis: temporal / memory
05

One sharp question a day

Turn consumption into thinking. The agent asks you something.

What — Alongside the digest, the agent poses one pointed question forced out of the day's material: "You've saved 4 agent-harness talks this month but fieldtheory has no harness abstraction — is that a gap or a deliberate no?" Answers get appended to a library/reflections.md — a private thinking log that itself becomes a source.

Why — Every other feature makes consuming easier. This one makes you synthesize. It's active recall — the single highest-leverage move for actually retaining what you read — and it flips the tool from output-only to a dialogue. Bold because it demands something of the user.

Basis: Same synthesis call and material; add a question to the output contract. Capture is a one-line ft daily --answer "…" append. Reflections feed back through the existing canonical index as a new source.
Effort: medSurprise: highaxis: feedback loop
06

Novelty score — suppress the echo

Is this genuinely new, or the sixth rehash? Rank accordingly.

What — Score each new item by distance from what you already hold. The 6th "train an LLM from scratch" repo gets folded into a one-line "+5 more like this," while a genuinely novel save gets a ⭐ and top billing. The digest stops treating the echo and the discovery as equals.

Why — Look at today's digest: the local-training theme lists six near-identical repos as if each were news. Signal drowns. A novelty gate is the cheapest way to make the digest denser and more honest about what actually moved.

Basis: FTS BM25 self-similarity (query the new item's terms against the pre-existing index; high overlap = echo) is pure SQL over canonical_bookmarks_fts — the same index connect.ts already uses. No embeddings required for v1.
Effort: medSurprise: medaxis: synthesis quality
07

The read-receipt loop (real interests, not word counts)

Learn what you actually open — kill the junk-token interest model.

What — Track which digest links you actually open/click, and feed engagement back into the interest model. Themes you consistently ignore get down-weighted; items you open define your real interests — replacing today's mechanical term-frequency (which thinks "stars," "code," and "tool" are your passions).

Why — interests.md is the one artifact in the pipeline that's actively misleading — it counts words, not attention, so its top signals are noise. A behavioral signal, however coarse, beats a term count. Highest payoff, but needs a surface that observes opens.

Basis: Needs a reader that can log opens — dovetails with the planned bookmarks web interface (docs/plans/2026-06-24-bookmarks-web-interface.md). Interim: a shell wrapper / ft open <id> that records the open before launching the URL. Interest model already regenerates each run in interests.ts — just add an engagement input.
Effort: med-highSurprise: medaxis: feedback loop

Stretch — high ceiling, thinner grounding

08

Ambient project-sliced primer

When you open a repo, inject only the digest slice relevant to that repo.

The north-star Layer 4. On entering any project session, surface just the recent-digest themes that touch this repo's Goal/Now/Next — plus a drift alert when your consumption diverges from what you're building ("heavy on local-training reads, none of your active repos touch it"). Extends projects-active.md + the second-brain recall skill from pull to push. Kept as stretch because the trigger surface (session-start hook injecting per-repo context) is the ambitious, less-proven part — but it's the direction everything else feeds.

Effort: highSurprise: medaxis: ambient recall
